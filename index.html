Welcome

I am a Ph.D. Student in Applied Economics at Virginia Tech's Department of Agricultural and Applied Economics, where I work under the supervision of Dr. Bradford Mills and Dr. Anubhab Gupta.

My research focuses on development economics, climate change economics, and gender and labor economics in agricultural contexts. Specifically, I examine how climate variability and extreme weather events affect agricultural productivity and farmer decision-making in Sub-Saharan Africa, with a particular focus on the Senegal Groundnut Basin.

My doctoral research investigates the economic impacts of extreme heat on groundnut production, land access dynamics, and youth engagement in agriculture. I employ econometric methods, remote sensing techniques, and spatial analysis to understand how environmental and economic factors shape agricultural outcomes and rural livelihoods.

Research

Research Interests

My research agenda centers on understanding the economic dimensions of climate change in agricultural systems, with particular emphasis on:

  • Climate Change Economics: Quantifying the economic costs of extreme weather events on agricultural productivity
  • Development Economics: Examining land access, resource allocation, and productivity in developing countries
  • Gender and Labor Economics: Analyzing gender disparities and youth engagement in agricultural production
  • Applied Econometrics and Remote Sensing: Using satellite data and spatial analysis to measure agricultural outcomes
